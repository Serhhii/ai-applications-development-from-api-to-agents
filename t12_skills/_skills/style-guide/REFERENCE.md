# Style Guide Reference

## Brand Voice at a Glance

| Attribute | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Voice | Conversational, clear, direct | Corporate, stiff, formal |
| Tone | Warm, confident, helpful | Cold, arrogant, apologetic |
| Sentences | Short. One idea each. | Long, clause-heavy run-ons |
| Vocabulary | Plain English, everyday words | Jargon, buzzwords, Latin phrases |
| Point of view | "you" for reader, "we" for us | Impersonal third person |
| Contractions | Always use them | "Do not", "It is", "We are" |

---

## Content Types

### Emails

**Subject lines**
- Keep under 50 characters
- Lead with the benefit or action, not the sender's name
- Avoid: "Important update", "Please read", vague teasers
- Use sentence case: "Your invoice is ready" not "Your Invoice Is Ready"

**Body**
- Open with the most important information — no warm-up preamble
- One clear CTA per email, placed near the end
- Sign off: "Thanks," or "Best," — never "Warm regards" or "Kind regards"
- Tone dial: transactional → neutral/efficient; marketing → upbeat; support → empathetic + solution-first

### Blog Posts

**Structure**
- Intro: 2–3 sentences max — hook + what the reader will get
- Headings: sentence case, outcome-focused (tell them what they'll learn, not just the topic)
- Sections: 150–300 words each

**Style**
- Reading level: Grade 8–10 (plain, accessible)
- Spell out acronyms on first use
- No heading should be a question unless the post answers it immediately

### Social Media

**LinkedIn**
- Max 1,300 characters (aim for under 800)
- Open with a bold first line (no "I'm excited to announce")
- Max 3 hashtags, placed at the end
- No emoji in professional announcements; 1–2 OK in casual posts

**Twitter / X**
- Max 280 characters
- Front-load the point — everything important in the first 100 chars
- Max 2 hashtags
- One emoji max, only if it adds meaning

### Support / Help Content

**Tone:** Empathy first, solution second.
- Start by acknowledging the problem before jumping to the fix
- Partnering phrases: "Let's fix this together", "Here's what to do next"

**Format**
- Use numbered steps for multi-step solutions
- Bold UI elements: click **Save**, select **Settings**
- Close with an offer to help further: "Let me know if this doesn't work and I'll look into it."

## Grammar & Mechanics

**Punctuation**
- Oxford comma: always ("red, white, and blue")
- Em dash (—): use for emphasis or to add a clause — like this. No spaces around it.
- Exclamation marks: max one per piece, and only when genuinely warranted

**Capitalization**
- Headings: sentence case only
- Product names: always capitalise as branded (e.g. Claude, Anthropic)
- Job titles: lowercase unless used as a proper title before a name

**Numbers**
- Spell out one through nine; use digits for 10 and above
- Always use digits for percentages (5%), money ($10), and measurements (3 km)

**Inclusive language**
- Use "they/them" as singular pronoun when gender is unknown
- Avoid: "guys", "mankind", "blacklist/whitelist" (use "blocklist/allowlist")
